How it works

Three steps from data to discovery

The system stores three simple facts about the world. Then it asks one question: do a drug and a disease share a biological target? If yes — that is a candidate worth investigating.

01

Map drug targets

Each drug is linked to the proteins and enzymes it affects inside the body — its biological targets.

02

Map disease targets

Each disease is linked to the same kind of proteins — the ones scientists know are involved in causing it.

03

Find the overlap

When a drug and a disease share a target, that is a signal. The more targets shared, the higher the score.

04

Rank by score

Score = shared targets ÷ total disease targets. Closer to 1.0 means a stronger biological connection.
